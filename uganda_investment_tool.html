üìã Business Registration Process

Register your business in Uganda through the streamlined process at URSB (Uganda Registration Services Bureau)
1

Name Reservation

Search and reserve your company name through URSB online portal or visit their offices. Name reservation is valid for 60 days. Cost: UGX 50,000

2

Prepare Required Documents

Memorandum & Articles of Association, Form 7 (application for incorporation), passport photos of directors, copies of IDs/passports, and proof of registered office address.

3

Submit Application

Submit documents to URSB with registration fees. Private company: UGX 175,000. Public company: UGX 250,000. Processing takes 1-3 working days.

4

Obtain Tax Identification Number (TIN)

Register with Uganda Revenue Authority (URA) for TIN. This can be done simultaneously with company registration. No additional cost.

5

NSSF Registration

Register with National Social Security Fund if you plan to employ staff. Required within 30 days of starting operations.

6

Business License

Obtain relevant business licenses from local authorities (KCCA for Kampala). Requirements vary by business type and location.

üìú Investment Licensing Process

Uganda Investment Authority (UIA) provides investment licenses and facilitation services for qualifying investments
1

Pre-Investment Consultation

Meet with UIA investment officers to discuss your project, understand requirements, and receive guidance on optimal investment structure.

2

Investment License Application

Submit completed application form with business plan, feasibility study, environmental impact assessment (if required), and proof of minimum investment threshold.

3

Document Review & Evaluation

UIA technical committee reviews application for completeness, viability, and compliance with investment criteria. Process takes 10-15 working days.

4

Investment Committee Approval

Qualifying applications are presented to the Investment Committee for final approval. Large investments may require Cabinet approval.

5

License Issuance

Upon approval, investment license is issued with specific terms, conditions, and incentives. License is valid for the project duration.

6

One-Stop-Center Services

UIA facilitates obtaining other required permits and licenses through their One-Stop-Center, streamlining the regulatory process.

üíº Minimum Investment Thresholds

  • Foreign investors: USD 100,000 minimum
  • Joint ventures (foreign + local): USD 50,000 minimum
  • Ugandan investors: No minimum threshold
  • Strategic sectors may have higher thresholds

üìä Uganda Investment Policies & Incentives

üéØ National Investment Policy Objectives

  • Transform Uganda into a modern, competitive upper middle-income country
  • Promote private sector-led economic growth and development
  • Attract quality foreign direct investment (FDI)
  • Develop industrial and technological capabilities
  • Create sustainable employment opportunities
  • Promote regional and export-oriented investments

üí∞ Investment Incentives

  • Corporate tax rate: 30% (competitive in the region)
  • Withholding tax on dividends: 15%
  • Capital gains tax exemption on securities listed on stock exchange
  • Accelerated depreciation allowances for industrial buildings and machinery
  • Investment deduction of 50% for specified investments
  • Duty exemption on machinery and equipment for licensed investments

üè≠ Priority Sectors with Enhanced Incentives

  • Agriculture and agro-processing: Value addition focus
  • Manufacturing: Import substitution and export promotion
  • Tourism: Infrastructure development and service provision
  • Information and Communication Technology (ICT)
  • Oil, gas, and mining: Upstream and downstream activities
  • Infrastructure development: Energy, transport, water

üåç Regional Integration Benefits

  • East African Community (EAC) common market access
  • Common Market for Eastern and Southern Africa (COMESA) benefits
  • African Growth and Opportunity Act (AGOA) eligibility
  • Everything But Arms (EBA) agreement with EU
  • Generalized System of Preferences (GSP) with various countries
  • African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA) participation

üîí Investment Protection

  • Constitutional guarantee against expropriation without compensation
  • Bilateral Investment Promotion and Protection Agreements (BIPPAs)
  • Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency (MIGA) membership
  • International Centre for Settlement of Investment Disputes (ICSID) signatory
  • Repatriation of profits and capital guaranteed
  • Non-discriminatory treatment of foreign investors

üìà Special Economic Zones (SEZs)
