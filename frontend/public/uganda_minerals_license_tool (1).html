⛏️ Uganda Minerals License Guide

Complete Guide for Mining License Applications in Uganda

Directorate of Geological Survey and Mines (DGSM)

🎯 Types of Minerals Licenses in Uganda

Uganda's minerals sector is regulated under the Mining Act 2003 and Mining Regulations 2004. The following licenses are available:

🔍 Exploration License

Grants exclusive rights to explore for minerals in a specified area. Valid for 3 years, renewable for 2 more years.

  • Maximum area: 1,000 sq km
  • Duration: 3 years + 2 years renewal
  • Exclusive exploration rights
  • Can be converted to mining lease

⛏️ Mining Lease

Provides exclusive rights to mine, extract, and dispose of minerals. Valid for 21 years, renewable.

  • Maximum area: 200 sq km
  • Duration: 21 years renewable
  • Full mining and extraction rights
  • Requires feasibility study

🏭 Mineral Processing License

Authorizes processing, refining, smelting, or beneficiation of minerals. Valid for 10 years.

  • For value addition activities
  • Duration: 10 years renewable
  • Can process own or purchased minerals
  • Requires environmental compliance

🚚 Mineral Dealer's License

Permits buying, selling, or dealing in minerals. Valid for 1 year, renewable annually.

  • For mineral trading activities
  • Duration: 1 year renewable
  • Requires registered business
  • Subject to tax obligations

📍 Location License

Temporary permit for small-scale prospecting and artisanal mining. Valid for 6 months.

  • Small-scale operations only
  • Duration: 6 months renewable
  • Limited to specific minerals
  • Cannot be held by companies

🔧 Mineral Rights for Building Materials

Special license for quarrying sand, stones, clay, and other construction materials.

  • Construction materials focus
  • Duration: 5 years renewable
  • Local government involvement
  • Environmental assessment required

📋 Minerals License Application Process

The application process varies by investor type and license category. All applications are processed by the Directorate of Geological Survey and Mines (DGSM) under the Ministry of Energy and Mineral Development.
1

Pre-Application Consultation

Duration: 1-2 weeks

Meet with DGSM officials to discuss your project, understand requirements, and get guidance on the most suitable license type. Foreign investors must also consult with Uganda Investment Authority (UIA).

Key Activities: Site visit planning, preliminary geological assessment, stakeholder identification

2

Document Preparation & Submission

Duration: 2-4 weeks

Prepare and submit complete application with all required documents. Applications can be submitted online through the DGSM portal or physically at their offices.

Key Documents: Application forms, company registration, technical reports, environmental assessment, financial statements

3

Initial Review & Payment

Duration: 1-2 weeks

DGSM conducts preliminary review of application for completeness. Upon satisfactory review, applicant is notified to pay required fees and charges.

Payment Methods: Bank deposit, mobile money, or online payment through approved channels

4

Technical Evaluation

Duration: 4-8 weeks

Detailed technical review by DGSM experts including geological assessment, environmental impact evaluation, and compliance verification. Site inspection may be conducted.

Evaluation Criteria: Technical competence, financial capacity, environmental compliance, local content requirements

5

Stakeholder Consultation

Duration: 2-4 weeks

Public notices published for stakeholder comments. Local community consultations conducted. Relevant government agencies consulted for clearances.

Stakeholders: Local communities, district officials, environmental authorities, other government agencies

6

License Committee Review

Duration: 2-3 weeks

Application reviewed by the Minerals License Committee comprising representatives from DGSM, NEMA, URA, and other relevant agencies.

Decision Options: Approve, approve with conditions, request additional information, or reject

7

License Issuance

Duration: 1-2 weeks

Upon approval, license is prepared and issued with specific terms and conditions. License agreement signed and registered.

Final Steps: License registration, gazette publication, commencement of operations

Total Processing Time: 3-6 months depending on license type and complexity
Validity Period: Varies by license type (6 months to 21 years)

📄 License Application Requirements

📋 General Requirements

  • Completed application form (Form ML-1)
  • Certificate of incorporation
  • Memorandum & Articles of Association
  • Board resolution authorizing application
  • Audited financial statements (last 3 years)
  • Tax clearance certificate
  • Work program and budget
  • Application fees payment receipt

🌍 Foreign Investor Additional Requirements

  • Uganda Investment Authority license
  • Certificate of good standing from home country
  • Apostilled company documents
  • Parent company guarantee (if applicable)
  • Local partnership agreement (if required)
  • Technology transfer agreement
  • Foreign exchange compliance certificate
  • Proof of minimum investment commitment

⛏️ Technical Requirements

  • Geological report and maps
  • Exploration or mining plan
  • Resource/reserve estimates
  • Feasibility study (for mining lease)
  • Environmental impact assessment
  • Mine closure and rehabilitation plan
  • Health and safety management plan
  • Community development agreement

👥 Personnel Requirements

  • Qualified Person (QP) certificate
  • Mine manager appointment
  • Safety officer designation
  • Environmental officer appointment
  • Proof of technical competence
  • Professional indemnity insurance
  • Training program for local staff
  • Local content compliance plan

💰 Financial Requirements

  • Bank guarantee or performance bond
  • Proof of financial capacity
  • Insurance coverage certificates
  • Rehabilitation fund establishment
  • Royalty payment guarantee
  • Environmental restoration bond
  • Working capital demonstration
  • Projected cash flow statements

🏛️ Legal & Compliance

  • Land acquisition agreements
  • Surface rights negotiation
  • Community consultation reports
  • Cultural heritage assessment
  • Water use permits
  • Environmental compliance certificate
  • Local government approvals
  • Dispute resolution mechanisms
Important: All foreign documents must be apostilled and translated into English. Technical reports must be prepared by qualified professionals registered in Uganda or their home countries.
